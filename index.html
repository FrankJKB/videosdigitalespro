Here are the most common photo and video formats that can be digitized.

35 mm Slide

The 35mm slide is the most common type of slide. Slide Mount: 2” x 2”. Transparency Film Size: 24mm x 36mm.

110 2”x2” Slide

Slide Mount: 2” x 2”. Transparency Film Size: 13mm x 17mm.

126 Slide

Slide Mount: 2” x 2”. Transparency Film Size: 28mm x 28mm.

127 Slide

Slide Mount: 2” x 2”.Transparency Film Size: 40mm x 40mm.

Super 127 Slide

Slide Mount: 2” x 2”. Transparency Film Size: nearly 2” x 2”.

Super 8 & 8mm Negatives

Negatives are usually on a strip of transparent plastic film. They come in different formats.

VHS Tapes

VHS (Video Home System) is one of the most common video format.

VHS-C Tapes

VHS-C uses standard VHS tape but in a smaller cassette housing.

MiniDV Tapes

Small DV (Digital Video) cassettes are called MiniDV. DV cassettes are available in other sizes.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Have a look at these FAQs to find answers to common questions.

  • How do I know how much footage I have on my tapes?

    We cannot determine the exact amount of recorded content on a videotape unless we play the entire tape through once. After the entire videotape has been recorded, we remove any blank space or blue screen content to ensure that the only recorded material is watchable.

  • Can I provide my own USB drive or hard drive to store the files?

    Yes, you can provide us your own drive and we will store your files on it. If you don't have a drive available, you can purchase a drive from us for an additional fee.

  • How do I know if my VHS tape is damaged?

    You may not be able to tell if your VHS tape is damaged until you digitize it. However, certain physical signs such as marks, cracks, or discolorations may indicate potential damage. We will perform a visual inspection of each tape before scanning to ensure that the process will not cause further damage.

  • How do I make sure my memories are safe during the scanning process?

    We understand that your memories are unique and important. That is why we take all necessary measures to ensure their safety during the scanning process. We handle your tapes, negatives and slides with care and work in a controlled environment to avoid exposure to dust or humidity.

  • How long does it take to digitize my memories?

    We do our best to complete your requests in less than a week. However, the delay also depends on the current demand. If we expect longer lead times, we will let you know in advance so you know what to expect. We work quickly yet meticulously to ensure that your files are digitized at the highest possible quality.

  • Can I choose the output format of my scanned files?

    We do not offer the option to choose your own output format at the moment. Video files will be saved in the H.264 format and photo files will be saved as JPEG. Those formats are very commonly used and easy to read on Mac or PC.
